02 · Motivation Statement
What EV4GH Means to Me (232 words)

Why I am applying

As a Nigerian biochemistry graduate working at the intersection of laboratory science, genomics, and digital health, I have become increasingly aware that generating evidence is not the same as influencing health systems. Through my work on antimicrobial resistance (AMR) in Salmonella Typhi and E. coli, I have seen how critical data on drug resistance often fails to translate into treatment guidelines or policy decisions. This gap is not merely technical. It is shaped by power, systems, and the disconnect between knowledge production and decision-making.

The EV4GH 2026 venture represents an opportunity to move beyond technical expertise and develop the skills required to engage with these broader dynamics. As a member of the Youth in Digital Health Network (YiDHC) under Africa CDC and Ambassador for the African Society for Laboratory Medicine, I have seen firsthand that advocacy and stakeholder engagement are as essential as evidence itself. I am drawn to EV4GH's focus on power-sensitive leadership and challenging dominant global health narratives, and motivated to contribute perspectives grounded in African health system realities.

Participation in EV4GH will strengthen my ability to translate genomic and data-driven insights into actionable health system reforms. It will equip me with the tools to engage policymakers, advocate for more inclusive and context-sensitive approaches to surveillance, and contribute to shaping more equitable global health agendas.

Ultimately, I see EV4GH not just as a learning opportunity, but as a platform to amplify my impact as a health systems actor committed to improving infectious disease surveillance and response in Nigeria and across Africa.

03 · Concept Note
What Global Health Problem I Want to Address

Strengthening Infectious Disease Surveillance in Africa Through Genomic Intelligence

HSR 2026 Theme: Platforms and Participation — Harnessing digital technologies and honouring social contracts

The Problem

Africa bears a disproportionate burden of infectious diseases including malaria, typhoid, and antimicrobial-resistant (AMR) infections, yet contributes only a small fraction of the genomic and surveillance data that informs global health policy. This is not simply a data scarcity problem, but a systems failure shaped by weak laboratory infrastructure, fragmented digital platforms, and global knowledge hierarchies that undervalue locally generated evidence.

In Nigeria, I have seen this disconnect firsthand. My research on Salmonella Typhi isolates identified that 63.5% carry dual quinolone resistance mutations, indicating a high likelihood of ciprofloxacin treatment failure. Similar gaps exist in my work on E. coli AMR prediction and malaria surveillance: data is generated, but not meaningfully integrated into the health system.

My Role and EV4GH's Contribution

As a member of the YiDHC under Africa CDC and Ambassador for ASLM, I actively contribute to conversations on diagnostic equity and digital health governance. A critical gap remains: navigating the power dynamics that shape whether evidence influences policy.

EV4GH 2026 offers a unique platform to develop the skills and networks needed to translate genomic intelligence into health system reform, and to ensure that African-generated data drives decisions in global health, not merely supports them.
